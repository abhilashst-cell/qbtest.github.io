Frequently asked questions

How does AI restore old and damaged photos?

AI models trained on millions of photo pairs learn to recognize common damage — tears, fading, scratches — and reconstruct the missing detail so the repaired area blends naturally with the rest of the image.

Can a badly torn or very faded photo be fully restored?

Most damage can be significantly improved. Results depend on how much of the original image survives — a photo that's mostly intact restores more completely than one that's severely faded or missing large sections.

Is online photo restoration free?

Yes, QuillBot's photo restoration tool is free to use, with no software to install and no professional retouching fees.

What is photo restoration and how does it work?

Photo restoration is the process of digitally repairing damage — tears, creases, fading, discoloration — in an old or worn photo. AI restoration tools analyze the image, identify damaged areas, and reconstruct them so the photo looks close to its original condition.

Can black-and-white photos be restored in color?

Yes. Alongside repairing damage, QuillBot's restoration tool can colorize black-and-white photos, adding natural-looking color to vintage prints in the same step.

How long does it take to restore an old photo online?

Most photos are restored in a matter of seconds once uploaded. More heavily damaged photos may take a little longer to process, but no manual retouching time is required.

Will restoring a photo remove handwritten notes or captions on it?

Restoration focuses on repairing damage like tears and fading rather than removing intentional content such as captions. If you want to remove specific marks, use a spot-removal or eraser tool after restoring.

What's the difference between photo restoration and photo enhancement?

Restoration repairs physical and visible damage — tears, creases, fading, discoloration. Enhancement improves overall image quality, such as sharpness, brightness, and resolution. Many restored photos benefit from both.
